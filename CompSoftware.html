The Ten Steps of Software Development


1. Programmer produces "bug-free" code.
2. Product is tested. Thirty bugs found.
3. Programmer fixes 10 of the bugs and explains to the testing department that actually the other 20 aren't really bugs...
4. Testing department finds that five of the fixes didn't work and discovers 18 new bugs.
5. GO TO Step 2 three times.
6. Product released with bugs intact, due to enormous pressure from marketing and sales and already launched ad campaign.
7. Users find 138 new bugs.
8. Original programmer no longer with company. New programming team fixes all but 3 of old bugs, introduces 296 more.
9. Product flops; programmers fired; new programmer hired to redo entire program.
10. Programmer produces "bug-free" code.
